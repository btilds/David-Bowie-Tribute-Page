Bowie's Life:

  • Jan. 8, 1947: David Bowie is born in Brixton, London.
  • 1962: Bowie forms his first band, the Konrads.
  • 1966: Jones officially assumes the name David Bowie, after previously trying out the stage name "Davy Jones," the same name as the Monkees' lead singer. Bowie chose the moniker after the frontiersman Jim Bowie, famous for his Bowie knife.
  • June 1967: Bowie releases his self-titled debut album, which (along with its first singles), fails to chart upon its initial release.
  • July 1969: Space Oddity is released as a single, several days before the Apollo 11 launch. Bowie's breakthrough hit, the song would go on to reach the Top 5 in the UK.
  • March 1970: Bowie marries his first wife, model and actress Angela Barnett.
  • Nov. 1970: Bowie releases The Man Who Sold the World, his first fully realized album, which transitioned the British folk of his earlier releases into a heavier rock sound, and also saw the singer playing with the androgynous image he'd help pioneer.
  • 20 best David Bowie songs of all time May 1971: Bowie and Barnett welcome a son, Duncan Zowie Haywood Jones.
  • June 1972: Bowie releases his landmark record The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars, a concept album that saw Bowie embracing a flamboyant glam-rock frontman status and cemented his popularity in the UK.
  • May 1974: Embracing a more funk-forward sound, Bowie releases Diamond Dogs, a dystopian concept album loosely based on George Orwell's 1984.
  • March 1975: Bowie achieves a new level of international acclaim with Young Americans, featuring the John Lennon collaboration Fame, which would become his first No. 1 single in the U.S.
  • May 1976: Bowie stars in the sci-fi film The Man Who Fell to Earth, playing an alien who journeys to Earth to save his extraterrestrial home planet. Loosely based on his role, Bowie debuts a new personality The White Duke, coinciding with the release of his album Station to Station.
  • Nov. 1975: Bowie becomes one of the first white artists to perform on the famous music/variety show Soul Train.
  • 1977-1979: Bowie releases the acclaimed "Berlin trilogy" of albums, recorded during his time in Germany: the Krautrock-influenced Low and Heroes (both released in 1977) and the more pop-oriented Lodger (1979).
  • Feb. 1980: Bowie and Barnett file for divorce.
  • Sept. 1980: Taking his talents to Broadway, Bowie stars in a production of The Elephant Man.
  • 1981: Bowie collaborates with Queen for the one-off single Under Pressure, which becomes his third No. 1 single in the UK.
  • 1984: Bowie gets his first Grammy, winning the Best Short Form Music Video category for his 20-minute film Jazzin' for Blue Jean, made to promote the single Blue Jean from his Tonight album.
  • June 1986: Bowie stars in Jim Henson's cult classic Labyrinth as Jareth, The Goblin King. Earlier that year, he also appeared in the British rock musical Absolute Beginners.
  • May 1989: Bowie and his new band Tin Machine release their self-titled debut.
  • 1992: After a two-year courtship, Bowie marries supermodel Iman.
  • 1996: Bowie is inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.

  • Jan. 8, 2013: On his 66th birthday, Bowie announces the release of The Next Day, his first studio album in a decade.
  • Jan. 8, 2016: Three years later, on his 69th birthday, Bowie releases his 25th and final studio album, Blackstar.
  • Jan 10, 2016: Bowie dies at the age of 69, after a battle with cancer kept secret from the public.

Hippie, glam rocker, androgynous icon, soulful White Duke, movie star, ordinary-guy-in-a-band, father, husband and a groundbreaking performer until the end; it's hard to think of a music icon who lived a fuller life than David Bowie.
